The 2% project

Founded in and hosted in 2022 by Badu Mentoring Group and the Black Excellence Network, Project 2% gives black undergraduates the opportunity to learn from black leaders who are thriving in their respective industries. The primary goal of project 2% is to provide a dynamic platform for black young individuals where they can aquire invaluable insights that will empower them to thrive in their chosen field.

The name came from a discovery made in 2020 that black people held less than 2% of leadership roles across a range of industries.

The conference includes: Masterclasses - These masterclasses are hosted by black leaders and attendees can choose which masterclass to attend based on the industry represented. this enables attendees to gain enlightenment on the black experience in various professional positions. Furthermore, there is the opportunity for attendees to ask questions. Keynote speakers - They provide exclusive wisdom on to excel in their chosen fields and advise on how to step into underrepresented fields. Networking - There is the opportunity to network with like minded young black people and this extends professional reach. Opportunities - This includes the opportunity to connect with sponsor firms and market stall companies. these companies can offer bespoke opportunities to attendees.

Event date: 7th September 2024

Internships

10,000 Black Interns

The "10,000 Black Interns" initiative is a UK-based program aimed at increasing the number of Black interns in various industries. Launched in 2020, it addresses the lack of diversity in the professional workforce by providing internships and work experience opportunities specifically for Black students and recent graduates.

The program is designed to help bridge the gap between education and employment by offering meaningful, paid internship placements across a range of sectors, including finance, law, engineering, and media. By partnering with numerous companies and organizations, the initiative seeks to create pathways for Black individuals to enter and succeed in different fields, while also fostering greater inclusion and representation within these industries.

In addition to internships, the program often includes support such as mentorship, networking opportunities, and career development workshops, aiming to equip participants with the skills and connections needed to advance their careers. The ultimate goal is to not only provide valuable work experience but also to challenge and change the systemic barriers that contribute to underrepresentation of Black professionals in the workforce.
